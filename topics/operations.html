    Operations

    Process design, flow, and capacity.

    Process view of a firm

    Operations transforms inputs into outputs through a network of activities 1. The basic primitives are flow units, resources, and buffers.

    Little's Law

    Little's Law Inventory (I) = Throughput (R) × Flow Time (T). A steady-state identity that holds for any stable process, regardless of distribution.

    Little's Law lets you infer any one of the three from the other two — e.g., measure throughput and inventory to back out average flow time2.

    Bottlenecks and capacity

    Process capacity = capacity of the bottleneck. Improving non-bottleneck resources does not raise output. This is the foundational insight of the Theory of Constraints.

    Five focusing steps

    1. Identify the bottleneck.
    2. Exploit it (squeeze every drop of capacity).
    3. Subordinate everything else to the bottleneck.
    4. Elevate the bottleneck (add capacity).
    5. Return to step 1 — the bottleneck has moved.

    Lean and Toyota Production System

    Lean aims to eliminate seven wastes (muda): overproduction, waiting, transport, over-processing, inventory, motion, defects3. Pull systems (kanban) replace push scheduling.
