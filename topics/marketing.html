    Marketing

    Segmentation, positioning, and the marketing mix.

    Strategy development cycle

    Marketing strategy is iterative. The seven stages below run as a loop — outputs of each stage feed the next, and the final stage feeds back into the first. Hover any stage for detail.

    STP framework

    Marketing strategy follows a sequence: Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning1. First, divide the market into homogeneous groups. Then choose which to serve. Finally, design a value proposition that occupies a distinct place in the target customer's mind.

    Positioning The act of designing a firm's offering and image to occupy a distinctive place in the minds of the target market.

    The 4 Ps (marketing mix)

    Customer Lifetime Value

    CLV is the present value of all future cash flows from a customer relationship. For a customer with annual margin m, retention rate r, and discount rate d: CLV ≈ m · r / (1 + d − r)2. Acquisition spend is justified only when CAC < CLV.

    The retention lever

    Because retention compounds, small improvements in r dramatically increase CLV. This is why most mature businesses prioritize churn reduction over net new acquisition.

    Brand equity

    Brand equity is the differential effect of brand knowledge on customer response to marketing3. It manifests as price premium, share resilience, and lower marketing elasticity.

    Margin notes

    1. Segmentation often fails by being demographic instead of behavioral. Christensen's "jobs to be done" reframes this around customer purpose.
    2. Formula assumes constant margin and retention. For real cohorts, use empirical retention curves rather than a fixed r.
    3. Keller's CBBE model: salience → performance/imagery → judgments/feelings → resonance.
