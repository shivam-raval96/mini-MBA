    Accounting

    Reading the three financial statements.

    The accounting equation

    Every transaction preserves Assets = Liabilities + Equity 1. The balance sheet is a snapshot at a moment; the income statement and cash flow statement explain what changed between snapshots.

    Balance Sheet Stocks at a point in time. Lists what the firm owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and the residual claim of owners (equity).
    Income Statement Flows over a period: revenue − expenses = net income. Accrual basis.
    Cash Flow Statement Reconciles net income to actual cash, split into operating, investing, and financing activities.

    Accrual vs cash accounting

    Accrual recognizes revenue when earned and expenses when incurred — not when cash moves. This better matches economic activity to periods but creates a gap between net income and cash flow2.

    DuPont decomposition

    ROE = Net margin × Asset turnover × Equity multiplier. This decomposition isolates whether returns come from profitability, efficiency, or leverage 3.

    Margin notes

    1. Double-entry bookkeeping enforces this identity automatically: every debit has an equal credit.
    2. A profitable firm can still go bankrupt if working capital growth consumes cash faster than operations produce it.
    3. High ROE driven mostly by leverage is fragile — it amplifies losses too.
